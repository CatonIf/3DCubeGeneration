Alphabet

The Knuk alphabet consists of the following 11 letters and 2 diacritical marks, with their relative romanization.
  • Voiceless plosives: ۹ (p) ۷ (t) ۲ (k)
  • Voiced plosives: ۴ (b) ۸ (d) ‎٦ (g)
  • Vowels: ‎٥ (u) ۱ (i) ۰ (a)
  • Modifiers: ۶ (n) ‎٤ (l)
  • Diacritics: ، (') ٫ (h)

Orthography

Consonants

There are strong consonants ⟨p t k⟩ and weak consonants ⟨b d g⟩.
Strong consonants are voiceless plosives (pup /pup/; tuk /tuk/), while weak consonants are weak consonants are voiced plosives (ubu /ubu/).
Weak consonants right after a strong consonants are voiceless fricatives (pbu /pɸu/; tgu /txu/).
Strong consonants right before another strong consonant become voiceless fricatives (uppu /uɸpu/; uktgu /uxtxu/).
Weak consonants right before another weak consonant become voiced fricatives (ubbu /uβbu/; udgu /uzgu/).
The letter ⟨n⟩ makes the preceding strong consonant a nasal (pnu /mu/).
The letter ⟨n⟩ before a weak consonant shifts to the consonant location (unbu /umbu/).

Vowels

Vowels can be either ⟨u⟩, ⟨i⟩, ⟨a⟩ or a diphthong formed by these.
⟨a⟩ is the longest vowel, while ⟨i⟩ is the shortest one, meaning that the diphthong ⟨ui⟩ will be pronounced /uj/ and not /wi/.

uuujwa

Phonotactics

Every word must stard with a voiceless plosive, voiceless cluster, nasal or just nothing.
All word-initial clusters
p /p/pb /pɸ/pd /ps/pg /px/pn /m/
t /t/tb /tɸ/td /ts/tg /tx/tn /n/
k /k/kb /kɸ/kd /ks/tg /kx/kn /ŋ/
or just nothing
This gets followed by a vowel.
This is the minimum that a word can be, so the possible shortest words are "u" (you), "i" (them), "a" (object).
Then there can be voiced clusters equivalents of the word-initial ones with vowels following them.
All mid-initial clusters
b /b/bb /βb/db /zb/gb /ɣb/nb /mb/
d /d/bd /βd/dd /zd/gd /ɣd/nd /nd/
g /g/bg /βg/dg /zg/gg /ɣg/ng /ŋg/
A word can end with a single strong consonant (p t k), or just nothing.
Word examples are: kgagat, punba, kaut, tak, tdiup.

Combining

Combining is made by simply putting words next to each other.
tak + tdiup = taktdiup
The ⟨h⟩ is for long vowels, which are used to indicate that the word combined ended in the vowel and not in the following plosive.
u + kaut = uhkaut
ukaut = uk + aut
When the plosive is in a cluster or a nasal, the ⟨h⟩ doesn't get added.
utdiup = u + tdiup
The ⟨h⟩ is also used between used for glottal stop /ʔ/ between vowels.
uahau = ua + au
